Financial Ratios

Net Profit Margin

The net profit margin reveals the percentage of revenue a company retains as profit after covering all expenses, taxes, and interest. It’s a critical measure of profitability, highlighting how effectively a company manages its costs relative to its revenue.

Current Ratio

The current ratio evaluates a company’s ability to meet its short-term obligations with its short-term assets. It is a key liquidity measure that indicates whether the company can sustain day-to-day operations and handle unexpected expenses.

Asset Turnover Ratio

The asset turnover ratio measures how efficiently a company utilizes its assets to generate revenue. It reflects the overall effectiveness of the company’s operations and how well it strategically manages its resources to produce sales.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio

The debt-to-equity ratio assesses a company’s financial leverage by comparing total debt to shareholders’ equity. It indicates how the company’s assets are financed through debt versus equity, giving insight into the company’s risk level.
